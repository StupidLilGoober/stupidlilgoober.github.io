I have decided to venture into a new corner of programming: **game development**.

Ok, well this isn't exactly new to me, but all of my previous attempts have gone
nowhere.

Now, I have a quarrel with game engines.

## The GUI

I feel that most engines are too dependent on their GUI. I don't personally dislike
GUIs, but I would prefer if they were an optional tool rather than the focus of the
engine.

> Btw, I know that some engines use a more file-focused design and that I could
> probably just make a CLI for them, but let's be honest, making a new engine is way 
> more interesting, and I would like being employed some day

Now this all seems fine and dandy, but then we have another problem.

## Creating this Hypothetical Engine

Nobody wants to do it, and that's completely fair. Programming a whole game engine
is a *huge* endeavor, and frankly I don't want to do it either. Or rather, I don't
want to do it all at once.

The way I'll handle it is by making multiple small games, and use all of the reusable
parts of these games to add to this engine. I believe this will be a far more fun and
rewarding way to make a game engine.

## About the Engine

There's not much I can say about what makes it unique since it quite literally
does not exist yet, but I plan on making:

* Lua scripting
* Heavy, optional moddability
* No heavy editor required

## The First Step

The first step, naturally, would be to make my first game.

I chose a precision platformer because it lets me focus on the fundamentals of a game engine 

---

This probably won't become a perfect engine, and that's not really the goal. The goal is 
to build something useful by making games, not by endlessly planning.
